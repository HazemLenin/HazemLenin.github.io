# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users
Two equally weighted audiences: recruiters and hiring managers evaluating a stack-versatile full-stack hire (deepest in .NET/Angular, productive in any stack), and prospective freelance clients sizing up Hazem for project work. Both are time-pressed and skimming — they need to gauge experience, skill, and credibility within seconds, then find a clear way to act.

## Product Purpose
A single-page portfolio site (Astro + Tailwind v4) that establishes Hazem Lenin as a product-minded full-stack engineer with 5+ years of experience. Success = a visitor downloads the CV; viewing project work is the proof-building fallback for visitors not yet ready.

## Positioning
A product-minded engineer: thinks in user outcomes, not tickets — engineering and product judgment in one hire.

## Conversion & proof
- Primary CTA: Download CV. Secondary: view projects (GitHub / live demos).
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "One product-minded engineer, end-to-end — outsized value in a single hire."
- Belief ladder: 1) real experience (5+ years, named employers) → 2) ships real products (projects and live demos) → 3) thinks in outcomes, not tickets → 4) acting is easy (CV one click away).
- Proof on hand: GitHub projects and live demos (Projects section), named employers (Experience section), certificates and degrees (Education section). No testimonials currently.

## Brand Personality
Sharp, credible, modern. Confident technical voice — no filler, no false modesty, no generic "passionate developer" language. Precision over decoration. Experience is stated as "5+ years" with no seniority level label — no "mid-senior", no self-assigned "senior".

## Anti-references
Generic AI-template look: cream/sand backgrounds, gradient text, tiny uppercase eyebrows above every section, numbered 01/02/03 scaffolding, hero-metric-card cliches, identical icon+heading+text card grids. Reject anything that reads as templated SaaS-landing-page filler rather than a considered personal site.

## Design Principles
- Show, don't tell — real project outcomes and specifics over adjectives like "passionate" or "innovative"
- Precision as a brand signal — tight typography, deliberate spacing, no filler sections
- One clear path to action — resume, project links, and contact should never be more than a scroll or click away
- Restraint over decoration — every visual choice should earn its place, not scaffold-by-reflex

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA baseline: ≥4.5:1 body text contrast, full keyboard navigation, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all motion (Lenis smooth scroll included).
